History

I have lived in Nyack practically my whole life. I live on in South Nyack on Gesner Avenue. The first history of Nyack was during 1675. The natives on the land were a group called the Nyacks. European settlers came and settled in what is now Rockland County. The European settlers farmed the land for about 125 years. Red sandstone and the river were important resources for the Nyack. Nyack grew exponentially and started shipbuilding and building boatyards for transportation. Around 1840, the town started building large factories, railroads, and local shops to draw commuters and shoppers.

Population

The population of Nyack is 7,185 people. It is the 20th largest populated town in Rockland County. The population by race is 62% white, 18% Hispanic or Latino, 27% Black or African American, 3% Asian, and 4% Native Hawaiian or Pacific Islander.

Rockland County

Nyack is located in Rockland County. Rockland county is located on the west side of the Hudson River in New York. The towns and cities in Rockland County are Bear Mountain, Blauvelt, Congers, Garnerville, Haverstraw, Hillburn, Monsey, Nanuet, New City, Nyack, Orangeburg, Palisades, Pearl River, Piermont, Pomona, Sloatsburg, Sparkill, Spring Valley, Stony Point, Suffern, Tallman, Tappan, Thiells, Tomkins Cove, Valley Cottage, West Haverstraw, and West Nyack.
